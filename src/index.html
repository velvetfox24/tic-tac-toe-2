Game Rules

Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe (Tic-Tac-Toe Lvl 2) is a strategic version of the classic game. It takes place on a 3x3 global grid of 9 local boards, making 81 total cells.

1. The Move Link Rule

Your move dictates where your opponent must play next. Whichever cell index you choose within a local board forces your opponent to play inside the corresponding local board index of the global grid.

Example: If you play in the top-right cell of the center board, your opponent's next move must be made anywhere inside the top-right local board.

2. Wildcard State (Free Choice)

If a player is directed to a local board that has already been won or is completely filled (draw), the targeting restriction is cleared. The player is granted a Free Choice wildcard and may make their move in any open cell on any active board.

3. Winning Local Boards

A local board is won by getting three marks in a row (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) on that board. Once won, that board is claimed and display a large overlay of the winning symbol.

4. Winning the Global Game

To win the entire game, you must win three local boards in a row (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) on the global grid.
